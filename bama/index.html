Barrie Area Malayalee Association (BAMA)

Welcome to the family of Barrie Area Malayali Association (BAMA). The association was established in 2022 by a group of passionate Malayalees from Barrie to bring all Keralites of Simcoe County together, connect and celebrate the cultural spirit of Malayalees. This secular, non-political, non-profit, socioâ€“cultural organization in the Simcoe County district, Province of Ontario, Canada, has almost 450 registered members. Simcoe County is located in the central portion of Southern Ontario, Canada which encompasses the following cities: Barrie, Innisfil, Orillia, Springwater, Wasaga Beach, Oro-Medonte, Elmvale, Collingwood, Midland, Angus, Beaverton, Bradford, Midhurst.

This Association aims to promote social, cultural, and charitable activities among the Malayalees. The Association tries to preserve and promote Indian culture, especially Kerala culture, through various festivals and events organized throughout the year. BAMA works for the socio-cultural well-being and growth of the Malayalee community holding on tight to the traditions of Kerala culture.

Inspired by the Sanskrit language, the word Kudumba translates to family and Barrie Area Malayali Association brings the force or power that holds the Malayali families together. On a broader perspective a group that works towards keeping our community together.
